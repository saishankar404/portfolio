Something's been bugging me about how people hype up so-called "AI" and the recent trends about how it'll change the job market and how it will replace developers and professionals instantly, and i don't agree to a certain level.

It sure will impact the job market but won't replace the work entirely.  
its a tool to make your work faster and less boring.

AI  - Artificial Intelligence, but is it truly intelligent? how do we define intelligence?  
AI is smart, sure, but I believe it's not intelligent yet.

but, why?

## Intelligence v/s Smart

Let's begin by defining how we define "intelligence" and "smartness", Intelligence is the ability to understand, reason, and learn , and being smart is being knowledgeable on the specific / range of tasks or subjects matter presented.

Smart is **sharp** or on **point**. Intelligent is **understanding** or **discerning**.

LLM's (_large language models_), often disguised as **AI** are trained on these huge chunks of data, information collected over a long period of time and trained to interconnect between each other to produce or present the info in a structured manner (_to explain in a layman language_).

Humans work(_observing the environment and taking it as an input data continuously_) in a similar way, but here's a twist, the human brain. its not trained on some **previous** data and are not just data-driven pattern recognizer.

Humans experience emotions, intuitions, dreams, and a deeper sense of meaning which the current ability of AI lacks.

## AI or AK?

Let's say we equip robots with sensors for heat, emotion, touch to help them perceive the world but they will still lack an innate sense of  
**right and wrong.**

It still doesn't know what to do when temperature of its metal body is rapidly increasing,

**_will it stay just like that under the heat?_**

or

**_will it move away just like how humans react?_**

its not possible to tell unless a human provides an instructions or set conditions.  
It is smart (_can sense the heat_) but not intelligent (_does nothing after realizing?_).

But keep in mind that intelligence is not just memory.  
Actually intelligence & memory/knowledge are **_complementary_**

It is not an **AI** actually, but **AK, _Artificial Knowledge_**.  
But this does not mean that the impact will not be huge on jobs, since for about up to 98% of tasks this interpolation-limited **_intelligence_** **_is good enough_**.

![AI Knowledge](/assets/blog/ai-knowledge.png)

## Instincts.

Every single **AI** today relies on training data and can't self-learn and iterate without the presence of former human knowledge.

A baby is born with a host of known skills and abilities it never learned from per-existing knowledge.  
An infant knows how to cry and when (its hungry, hurt, scared) without ever seeing another baby cry.

That's **real intelligence**.

Thing is all animals poses instinct to do things without seeing it first.. A deer knows to run when it senses a predator, birds migrate without being taught, and a dog can sense danger or emotions in humans.  

These are forms of intelligence that AI simply doesn't have _yet._

Humans control these systems and are responsible for improving them. The system itself isn't improving on its own.

It constantly has to be adjusted by humans.  
i.e. prompting (_you giving inputs to AI to do some task_)

LLMs can create novel permutations of existing ideas, but it currently takes a human to recognize them as being useful.  
AI can produce new equations sure, but can it say where do we apply it?  
No. it still takes a human to identify where to apply that equation.

## **AI is making us dumber.**

Yup, ironically instead of AI making us smarter, studies suggests that  
it's making us dumber.  
You may have noticed in past few months how [**ChatGPT**](https://chatgpt.com/) and [**Deepseek**](https://chat.deepseek.com/) has taken over the internet by storm with all of the trends because of their capabilities.

When faced with a problem, we no longer think critically.  
And we seek to these LLMs often-called as **AI** for answering the question almost instantly and we don't think about the problem at all in the first place.  
Sure, it does give the answer but **how we reach the answer** is  
more **important than the answer itself**.

why? because _it is the_ **_best_** _way to learn._

we explore 10 different ways to solve that problem and we learn each of it and choose which one is the best for us.  
We don't reach the absolute dead-end and think about all the possibilities and the edge-cases of the question.

we just go to [**chat.com**](https://chat.com/) and copy-paste whatever errors we face and sometimes we don't even see the problem at the first place.

why? because It's **convenient** and **_quick_**.

![Knowledge vs Speed](/assets/blog/knowledge-speed.png)

But if we stop thinking for ourselves, who is truly in control: **_us or AI?_**

## **Replicating Human Brain.**

Even if we manage to create the real intelligence by reverse engineering brain and somehow applying to machines.

AI will still face limitation, just as humans do.  
because it's literally a human brain.

you can't surpass that or maybe the machine wont let you.  
because now it has the real intelligence.

In reality, machines are nothing more than electrical signals converted based on its voltage to a binary code via a series of gates that can be understood by rudimentary processors.

The human brain is incredibly **_complex_** and we've only understood  very less % about how it truly functions.

Evolutionary biology tells us that there's living change to these systems which respond to external stimuli.

That must also be reverse engineered in order to understand DNA, in order to build a brain.

AI could be _intelligent_ if we replicate the human brain capabilities to a machine.

There's 3 **primary things** which need to happen before we can fully connect with a **machine at a brain-interface level**:

1. **fully map out the human brain.**
2. **solve the bandwidth problem of signal to and from brain and machine _(quantum)_**
3. **down-sized computing system small enough to work with the brain**

aka [**neuralink**](https://neuralink.com/).

## Solving using Quantum Computing?

Quantum mechanics provides us a means to do things otherwise impossible in traditional physics as it is understood today.

I believe that its the key to unlocking the next technical age and elevating our civilization, such as it was elevated in the agricultural and industrial revolution.

Until we can solve for this we're bound by the physical limitations of silicon **_(silicon-based computing)._**

And as we can see, especially evidenced by the last decade,  
hardware improvements have been very **minute**.

Blame that on us approaching the **limitations of _silicon_** and its relationship with **_heat_** and **_resistance_**. We need to further refine **_graphene structures_** and **_superconductors_** to push AI forward. (_that for another blog_)

constraints?

no, **_energy_** isn't the constraint.  
the constraint is **_material science and physics._**

we do have the energy (called **_nuclear_**). we don't have the tech _yet_.

We'd have virtually no problems in terms of energy production and its harmful bi-products had we as a society not allowed ignorance and fear to pull us away.

Every single nuclear disaster was easily avoidable in half a dozen ways. The modern technology for nuclear reactors has solved for these problems.

> It's always fear and ignorance which lead mankind astray.

## Final note: looking forward

What LLMs can be good for, is to serve as knowledge/routine stores for an actual AGI _(basically AI with intelligence and extra ordinary capabilities)._  
They're a memory — a representation of a data corpus — and memory is a necessary component of intelligence.
